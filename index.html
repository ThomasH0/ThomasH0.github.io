The Team

Walter Avarelli

Born June 3rd, 1912 and died in 1987. Born in Rome and became a judge for the sport. He took up bridge during World War II when more than thirty years old. He was fascinated with gastronomy, the study of the relationship between food and culture. He was a longtime partner of Giorgio Belladonna using the Roman Club bidding system. They worked together to improve the system together and presented their findings in a book.



Giorgio Belladonna

Born June 7th 1923 and died May 12th 1995 was considered the greatest Italian bridge player of all time. He won 16 world championship titles with Blue Team playing with his longtime partner Walter Avarelli between 1956 and 1969 then later with Benito Garozzo. He was the lead inventor behind the Roman Club bidding system. He died of lung cancer on May 12th 1995 in Rome.



Pietro Forquet

Born 1925 and still alive today is considered one of the greatest Italian bridge players of all time, with most of the team members. Not only was he considered for being one of the greatest bridge players he also was highly regarded for having nerves of steel. He helped write the book on the Blue Club bidding system which resulted in the 1950s Neapolitan system to be modified by their partnership. He also wrote another book called Bridge with the Blue Team that held all of the blue team's collection of deals.



Benito Garozzo

Born September 5th 1927 and still alive today as well. He started in 1961 with the Italian Blue Team as a last minute substitute for the Bermuda Bowl. After that he would play in regular partnerships with Pietro Forquet as a starter all the way to 1972 then with Giorgio Belladonna. His whole life leading up to his career in the Blue Team mainly revolved around playing cards or various types of board games, it was his niche and he was good at it.



History

Blue Team (Italian: “Squadra Azzurra”) represented Italy in international contract bridge tournaments winning sixteen world titles between 1957 to 1969 Three regular pairs: Walter Avarelli, Giorgio Belladonna, Pietro Forquet, Benito Garozzo, and Massimo D’Alelio. The spiritual father, long-time coach, and non-playing captain to 1966 was Carl’Alberto Perroux

The United States was considered the best in the world after the war. Captain Perroux would undergo long-term preparations to win.

In 1956 Blue Team won their first bowl defeating the champion France in Stockholm allowing them to contest the 1957 Bermuda Bowl in New York City where they defeated a United States team. The players were Walter Avarelli, Giorgio Belladonna, Eugenio Chiaradia, Massimo d’Alelio, Pietro Forquet, and Gulielmo Siniscalco. They continued to reign, winning twelve world team championships in their thirteen years from 1957 to 1969. Blue Team retired after 1969 but key members would return to win four more world titles between 1972 and 1975.

Most of their success was due to the bidding systems, teams would complain that the Italians had an unfair advantage due to them using bids that were partly incomprehensible to Americans.
